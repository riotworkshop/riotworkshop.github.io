R is a programming language for statistical computing, with thousands of packages available in open-source repositories and over 2 million users in both academia and industry.

RIOT 2020 is a one-day workshop dedicated to exploring future directions for the development of R language implementations, tools, and R extensions. The goals of the workshop include, but are not limited to, sharing experience of developing different R language implementations and tools and evaluate their status, exploring possibilities for increasing involvement of the R users community in the efforts of constructing different R implementations, identifying R language development and tooling opportunities enabled by the emerging implementations, and discussing future directions for the R language. The workshop will consist of a number of short talks and discussions and will bring together developers of R language implementations, tools, and extensions.

Workshop topics include

  • R language tooling support
  • experience reports and technical details of novel R extensions, for example, ALTREP implementations
  • novel R language implementation techniques
  • R language optimizations
  • progress reports on the status of emerging R language implementations
  • R language implementation experiences

One page abstracts describing talks topics should be submitted via email to Stepan Sindelar by May 1st, 2020 (11.59pm AoE). We will send a confirmation upon receiving the abstract and accepted talks will be announced on May 8th.
